About the Trainer

Meet Your Coach

I came from culinary arts.

Fitness wasn't on my radar. I wasn't unhealthy — just not someone who thought about training as a career, or a calling.

Then my brother invited me to work out with a close friend before a night out. I agreed without thinking. That workout slaughtered me. The next morning I could barely lift my arms.

I told myself: never again. I'd never be defeated like that again.

So I started — slowly, at home. And somewhere in those early months, something shifted. I got stronger than I'd ever been. More mobile. Pain-free in ways I hadn't noticed I wasn't. I wanted more of it. And I realized I wanted to help other people find the same thing.

That was fifteen years ago.

Since then I've worked with seniors and collegiate athletes, former pros and people who just want to move without hurting. In those fifteen years, three things have proven true every single time.

We're working against age and time. The older we get, the harder it is to get in shape, stay in shape, or get back to it. That's not pessimism — it's why starting now always beats waiting.

Foundation comes before everything else. Strength is being stable in motion. Not just lifting heavy — moving well, consistently, without falling apart. Slow and steady isn't a consolation prize. It's the strategy.

Pain isn't weakness. Pain is a message. Ignore it long enough and it becomes the whole story.

That's the work. Not dramatic. Not fast. But real — and built to hold up.
